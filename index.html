John D. Morgan, Ph.D., GISP

I am an Associate Professor and long-standing core faculty member in the MS GIS Administration program for over a decade at the University of West Florida. Prior to academia, I worked in industry and public-sector technology roles including systems engineering, web development, and GIS application development for the Florida House of Representatives’ Redistricting Committee. I earned my PhD in Geography from Florida State University and have held research and teaching appointments at Florida State University, UNC Asheville, and Western Carolina University. My teaching and mentorship emphasize computational rigor, spatial thinking, and professional preparation for careers in government, emergency management, and environmental analysis.

My research advances computational time geography and spatial decision science, with a focus on how environmental change, regulatory systems, and infrastructure reshape access, visibility, and spatial rights. I develop spatially explicit models that integrate environmental transformation, institutional response, and constraint reconfiguration to better understand competing forms of accessibility across dynamic landscapes, particularly in coastal systems.

Methodologically, my work integrates GIScience, geospatial computing, and probabilistic modeling, including Bayesian network integration with GIS, spatial databases, Python-based analytical workflows, and machine learning approaches. I develop scalable spatial data pipelines and satellite-derived datasets to support uncertainty-aware decision modeling in environmental risk and infrastructure systems.

I also maintain applied research programs in hazard mapping, spatial criminology, and legal geography. These projects examine wildfire dynamics, crime patterns, environmental enforcement, and property access using time-geographic analysis, GeoAI, participatory mapping, and advanced viewshed modeling. Across all areas, cartography and geovisualization serve as both analytical engines and tools for communicating complex spatial trade-offs to policy and public audiences.

I continue to maintain my Geographic Information Systems Professional (GISP) credentials. Also, I am proud to have published three chapters in the UCGIS Geographic Information Science & Technology Body of Knowledge (BoK), such as the topics of spatial joins and crime mapping.
