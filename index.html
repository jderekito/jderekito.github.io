    John D. Morgan, Ph.D., GISP

    I am an associate professor in the MS GIS Admin program at the University of West Florida. I am also an adjunct professor of GIS in Western Carolina University's Emergency and Disaster Management program.

    My interdisciplinary research interests span the social and environmental sciences. Most of my work applies to urban planning and public safety. My research methods include cartography and map design (e.g., user-driven map UI/UX design and participatory GIS) and spatial data science (e.g., spatial autoregression, GeoAI, etc.). Specific research topics have focused on novel cartographic methods to address the challenge of mobility in crime and how to spatially explore the tensions between hazards (e.g., hurricanes) and land use (e.g., viewshed amenities).

    While serving as an Aviation Electrician in the US Marine Reserves, I completed my Bachelor of Arts in Economics with a minor in Applied Computer Science (UCF). Entering the professional workforce during dot-com boom of the late-1990s, I became a computer applications programmer working in private industry and even returned to academia, completing my Master of Science in Management Information Systems (UCF). Eventually, I completed my doctoral degree in geography while working as an information technology manager for FSU'sÂ International Programs. After my doctoral studies, I worked as a senior research scientist/applied software designer at UNC Asheville's National Environmental Modeling and Analysis Center (NEMAC), building full-stack Web GIS solutions for clients like the USDA Forest Service.

    I continue to maintain my Geographic Information Systems Professional (GISP) credentials. Also, I am proud to have published three chapters in the UCGIS Geographic Information Science & Technology Body of Knowledge (BoK), such as the topics of spatial joins and crime mapping.
