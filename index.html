    I am a (spatial) data scientist, occasional cartographer, and mostly associate professor of GIS with interdisciplinary research interests across the social and environmental sciences. Most of my work has applications to urban planning and public safety. My methods integrate GIS and data science, drawing on my 18 years of IT/GIS industry experience. Specifically, I occasionally use R, Python and SQL for spatial analysis. From a professional career trajectory perspective, I paid my way through college by taking out loans and serving in the US Marine Reserves (GI Bill). After college, I became a computer applications programmer. After all, I had to repay those loans.
